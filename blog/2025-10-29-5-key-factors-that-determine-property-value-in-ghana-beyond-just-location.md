"What is my property *really* worth?" It's the most common question in real estate, whether you're securing a mortgage, preparing for a sale, or assessing assets for your business.

While "location, location, location" is the famous answer, a professional valuation is a complex assessment that goes much deeper. As certified valuers based in Sunyani, we see clients overestimate or underestimate their property's value daily by overlooking crucial factors.

Here are the 5 key factors that professionally determine a property's value in Ghana.



### 1. Location & Neighbourhood



This is still the most important factor, but it's more than just a "good area." We analyse:

* Accessibility: Proximity to major roads, markets, business districts, schools, and hospitals.
* Infrastructure: The quality of the road network, drainage systems, water supply, and electricity.
* Amenity: Availability of social amenities like parks, shops, and security.
* Risk: Is the area prone to flooding? Is it near a hazardous site? These factors significantly decrease value.
* Zoning: The land's designated use (residential, commercial, mixed-use) dictates its potential and, therefore, its value.



### 2. Condition, Age & Quality of Finish



A new building is not always more valuable than an old one if the old one is built to a higher standard. We assess:

* Structural Integrity: The state of the foundation, walls, and roof. A property with visible cracks or a leaking roof will see its value plummet.
* Age & Depreciation: All buildings wear out. We calculate the "effective age" (how old it *appears* and *functions*) versus its chronological age.
* Quality of Finishes: The materials used for flooring (e.g., porcelain vs. ceramic tiles), kitchen fittings, plumbing fixtures, and windows are critical value indicators. High-quality finishes command a higher price.
* Maintenance: A well-maintained property retains its value far better than one suffering from neglect.



### 3. Land Title & Documentation



This is a critical factor in Ghana. A property with flawed or non-existent documentation is a high-risk liability, not an asset.

* Clear Title: A property with a registered Land Title Certificate is significantly more valuable than one with just an Indenture or, worse, no documentation.
* Bankability: Lenders and financial institutions will not accept a property as collateral for a mortgage without a clean, registered title. This directly impacts its marketability and value.
* Lease Term: The remaining term on the lease (e.g., 50 years left vs. 90 years left) directly influences the value, especially for commercial properties.

### 4. Property Size & Usable Space



Size matters, but so does design. We measure:

* Plot Size: The total land area (e.g., 70ft x 100ft).
* Gross Floor Area (GFA): The total size of the building itself.
* Layout & Functionality: It's not just about the number of bedrooms, but how well the space is used. A well-designed 3-bedroom house with good airflow and natural light can be more valuable than a poorly designed 4-bedroom house with dark, unusable rooms.

### 5. Current Market & Economic Conditions



A property is only worth what the market is willing to pay *at that specific time*. We must analyse external factors:

* Supply and Demand: How many similar properties are for sale in the Sunyani area right now? If there's a surplus, prices may be stagnant. If there's high demand, prices rise.
* Comparable Sales: We analyse the *actual recent sale prices* of similar properties in the neighbourhood. This is the most reliable indicator of current market value.
* Economic Climate: Broader issues like interest rates (cost of mortgages), inflation, and overall economic stability heavily influence the property market.
